About me

I am a PhD student in computational neuroscience at the University of Oxford in the Tim Vogels group. I am interested in using mathematical techniques to study the brain. During my PhD, I have studied recurrent neuronal-network models of cortical motor circuits. We found that changing the input-output sensitivity of neurons (called gain modulation) provides a powerful mechanism for controlling both the shape and duration of neural activity. Before my PhD, I worked as a research statistician at the UK Ministry of Defence. I obtained a BSc in mathematics from the University of East Anglia and an MSc in applied mathematics from Imperial College London.
